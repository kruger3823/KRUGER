Chameleon


Dating apps were his hunting grounds, and patience was his most effective strategy. Past relationships had been traumatic as his loved ones consistently desired different personas from him. Post-college life for him was an exercise in shapeshifting. He would chat for days, asking the right questions, letting them paint a portrait of the perfect man. When the day of the first date finally arrived, he would step into the restaurant not as a stranger, but as the living, breathing manifestation of their checklist. He didn't just meet their expectations; he became them.

The chameleon’s colours work perfectly—until they don't. Five women in two years had walked straight into his meticulously crafted mirrors. Five times he had played the saviour, the rebel, the artist, or the anchor, donning their desires like a tailored suit. He had mastered the art of the perfect fit, yet each time, the illusion eventually faded, leaving him empty-handed. He was a master actor waiting for a script that mattered.

Then, she arrived.
She wasn’t a puzzle to be solved; she was an El Dorado with no map.
For days, he deployed his usual tactics on the screen, throwing out his finely tuned lines, waiting for her to lay down the blueprints of her ideal man. But she gave him nothing. Her answers were sharp, guarded, and frustratingly chaotic. She didn't want a saviour, she mocked the rebels, and she saw right through the anchors. She was entirely, unapologetically herself, refusing to project a checklist for him to steal.
When the night came to finally meet her in the flesh, the maestro found himself paralysed before the curtain even rose. For the first time in his life, he stood outside the dimly lit lounge with absolutely no character to step into.

He couldn't find the angle. He couldn't map the trap.
To win her, he couldn't play a part—but after years of being everyone else, he had forgotten how to look in the mirror and see himself. He was about to walk into a date entirely unarmed, stripped of his disguises, facing the terrifying reality that the only card left to play was the one he had spent a lifetime hiding: the truth.


The chameleon was fracturing.

He knew she was the one—the ultimate prize, the only woman who mattered—which meant the truth was absolutely out of the question. If she saw the hollow man behind the curtain, she would vanish. So, he stayed in the game. But without a blueprint, he was building a house in a hurricane.
Whenever she threw tantrums, the system crashed. She would unleash a sudden, chaotic storm of emotion, and instead of smoothly adapting, he would freeze. His mind would spin through a rolodex of stolen personalities—Should I be the stoic anchor? The toxic bad boy? The empathetic listener?—but none of them fit the lock. He was glitching in real-time, offering a stuttering patchwork of conflicting reactions while she watched him with narrowing, suspicious eyes. The pressure was suffocating. Being around her was a high-wire act over a pit of broken glass, leaving him utterly devastated, physically drained, and drowning in stress.
Then came the night the mask didn't just slip—it shattered.
The shadows of his bedroom were thick, heavy with the scent of rain and rain-soaked pavement outside. They were tangled in the sheets, a desperate, heated collision born of a week's worth of tension. The momentum was building, the air thick with anticipation, and then, abruptly, the music stopped.
She paused, looking up at him through the dark, waiting for him to take the lead.
And in that split second of silence, panic struck like lightning.
His mind went completely blank, then violently flooded with the ghosts of his past performances. He was suspended over her, paralysed by a agonizing choice, and he couldn't remember which lie he had told her.
What did she want? What character was he playing tonight?
Did he need to dominate her, to catch her jaw and slap her hard against the mattress to satisfy a craving for danger? Or was he supposed to devour her gently, a tender, poetic lover worshipping her skin in the dark? Or worse—had he spun a web where he was the innocent, hesitant first-timer, letting her take control?
The seconds stretched into an eternity. The gears in his head ground together with an audible, terrifying friction. He looked down at her eyes reflecting the dim city lights outside, and he realised he had no idea who he was supposed to be.
"Hey," she whispered, her voice cutting through the dark, laced with a sudden, jarring confusion. "What are you doing? Why are you just... staring at me?"

He forced a ragged wince, rolling onto his back with a hand pressed to his forehead.
"I'm sorry," he breathed, letting his voice strain with a manufactured vulnerability. "My head. That migraine from earlier just hit me like a wall. Everything went completely white."
It was a flawless redirection. The suspicion in her eyes instantly dissolved into quiet concern.

That night, staring at the ceiling as the heavy Kochi rain beat against the glass, he knew the game was over. The blueprint was gone. The machine had glitched, and if he didn’t fix the gears, the next crash would be fatal.
While she slept soundly beside him, the blue light of his phone illuminated his sweating face. His fingers flew across the screen, desperately searching through the city's private practices, bypassing the generic family counsellors until he found what he needed: a high-profile clinical psychologist tucked away in a quiet, unassuming corner of Panampilly Nagar.
The profile promised absolute discretion. He booked the earliest available session under a fake name, staring at the confirmation screen as a cold realisation settled in his chest. For the first time in his life, he wasn't looking for a script to play a character—he was paying a stranger to help him find the man he had buried underneath them all.


The Panampilly Nagar office smelled of old paper and leather, a quiet sanctuary meant to strip away defences. But the moment the renowned therapist leaned forward, a strange, electric current shot through his veins.
He had expected an interrogation. Instead, he felt the familiar, intoxicating rush of the hunt.
Before she could even ask her first deep question, his instincts took the wheel. He looked at her sharp suit, the analytical tilt of her head, the precise way she held her pen. His mind didn't freeze this time—the machine clicked perfectly into place. He pulled a script from his past, effortlessly stepping into the role of the tragic, self-aware intellectual drowning in existential dread.
The doctor didn’t see through him. She leaned back, nodding, captivated by the flawless performance he was laying at her feet.
A wave of profound, intoxicating comfort washed over him. In front of the city's best mind, he wasn't a broken patient; he was the puppet master again. He knew exactly which lines to say, which pauses to take, and which vulnerabilities to fake. He had come to find himself, but as he spun his web around the therapist, he realised he didn't want to be cured. He just wanted a bigger stage.

The next day, he went back to his old hunting grounds. He reached out to the women from his past—the ones he had already mapped, measured, and mastered.
Stepping back into those old scripts was like sliding into a well-worn leather jacket. With one, he was the brooding artist; with another, the dependable confidant. He spent hours toying with them, pulling their strings from behind a screen, watching them react exactly as he predicted. The absolute control was an intoxicating relief. The glitching stopped. The static in his brain cleared, replaced by the smooth, quiet hum of a perfectly calibrated machine. He was the puppet master again.
And then, in the safety of that high, the intrusive thought took root.
He loved his current girl—she was the only prize that truly mattered. But she was a storm he couldn't control, a constant threat to his sanity. Why not split the difference? He could use his old targets as a release valve, a place to relieve the suffocating stress of his real life. By bleeding off his anxiety into these old games, he could return to his girl with a clear head, perfectly calm, acting casual and effortless while maintaining the mask.
He didn't need to fix himself. He just needed to balance the books.

Managing a network of past conquests while trying to anchor a volatile relationship at home required a precision he could no longer maintain. A missed call, a delayed text, a vague alibi—the strain was starting to show.
Simultaneously, her reality broke the fantasy. A severe flare-up of her PCOD brought intense hormonal shifts and unpredictable mood swings that quietly starved their physical intimacy. The casual, effortless persona he had designed to keep her calm did the exact opposite. It became a beacon. Her suspicions grew into a quiet, dangerous vigilance. The chameleon was running out of room to blend in, and the trap was closing.

To his girlfriend, who was currently drowning in severe stress and failing health, this relocation felt like a literal lifesaver. She was absolutely thrilled, clinging to the hope of Dubai as her ultimate escape, completely unaware that the man she loved was using her desperate need for healing as the perfect smokescreen to mask his final, deadly moves.


With the Dubai tickets and a new life booked, he turned all his dark energy toward his final act. His fifth and last target was a popular influencer, a high-profile figure whose digital footprint was massive but whose real life was heavily guarded. Getting close to someone with thousands of followers wouldn't be easy, so he had to play the part of a wealthy client. He used one of the anonymous, stolen burner phones he had bought at the Broadway market, set up a fake Telegram account, and reached out to the influencer, proposing a high-end couple's photoshoot at a secluded location.
For two agonising days, the chat window remained silent. The chameleon waited in the shadows, the voices in his head growing louder as the police and the private investigator closed in.
Then, the stolen phone buzzed. It was a call from the influencer. The chameleon used his most polished, professional voice, spinning a tale of a luxury brand launch. To completely lock in the trap and eliminate any doubt, he immediately gave an advance payment of 1 lakh INR. The massive sum worked like a charm. Convinced by the money, the influencer agreed to the gig and sent over the final, confirmed location for the shoot—a remote, scenic spot perfect for a photograph, and even more perfect for a murder.

The influencer arrived at the remote location, completely unaware of the trap. Stepping out of his vehicle, he was met by a striking figure—the chameleon, heavily disguised as a transgender art photographer, carrying professional gear and speaking with absolute creative authority. To establish trust, the chameleon spoke at length about his background in wildlife photography and his desire to break into raw, conceptual art. He pitched a dramatic, avant-garde concept for the shoot: a dark, emotional scene where a man appears to hang himself while his wife weeps at his feet. The influencer, captivated by the intense artistic vision and the 1 lakh advance already in his pocket, agreed to pose for the setup.
But as the props were placed and the tension peaked, the camera angles ceased to matter. With the influencer positioned exactly where he wanted him, the chameleon slowly shed the disguise. The polished persona melted away, revealing the true, terrifying face underneath. He turned to the influencer's wife, who had accompanied him to the shoot, and confronted her directly. In that chilling moment, he laid bare his real, horrifying intentions, bringing his twisted ritual to its ultimate, catastrophic climax.

Just as the blade glinted in the light, poised to strike, the air was shattered by the arrival of his own girlfriend, who  emerged from the shadows of a nearby building. With a roar of betrayal, she slapped him repeatedly, her fury acting as the catalyst that brought the hidden cavalry—the investigator and police—swarming into the scene. In a chaotic blink of an eye, the knife was kicked away and he was wrestled to the ground in handcuffs. As he lay pinned to the earth, the absurdity of his demise became his only reality: his girlfriend was weeping in utter heartbreak over the monster he had become, while the influencer and his wife stood nearby, laughing at the sheer pathetic nature of the man who had tried to play God. He stared up at them, his mind completely fractured, unable to process the collapse of his perfect, curated existence; he was no longer the mastermind, but a broken, trembling clown, finally seeing that his grand final act was nothing more than a hollow, tragic joke.


The overhead fan in the interrogation room cut through the heavy silence with a rhythmic, metallic scrape. The investigator leaned forward, the harsh fluorescent light casting deep shadows across his face. He placed his palms flat on the scarred wooden table, locked eyes with the suspect, and let out a long, slow breath.
"Reality is a bitter pill, isn't it?" the investigator began, his voice dropping to a low, gravelly rumble. "You thought you were the director of this little tragic play. But let me show you exactly where your script fell apart."

And that desperate move burned a neon sign right over your head."

His chameleon mind, backed into a corner, discarded the fear. It discarded the panic. Instead, it clawed through its mental wardrobe and pulled out the final mask: the hyper-energetic, venomous cynic.
Suddenly, the heavy atmosphere evaporated. A high, sharp laugh rattled the cinderblock walls as he leaned forward, eyes wide and dancing with an erratic, terrifying energy.

"You want a story, Inspector? Let me tell you a love story!" he chirped, his voice cutting through the room like a siren, hands gesturing wildly despite the heavy clink of the chains.
"Once upon a time, there was a boy. An ordinary boy. And along came a girl who decided he was her project. Day and night, night and day, she talked, and talked, and talked! She moulded him. The investigator, momentarily paralysed by the sheer whiplash of the performance, signalled to the guard. The guard turned, took two steps toward the water dispenser outside, and unlatched the heavy iron door.
That split second of shifted focus was all the chameleon needed.
With the explosive, terrifying speed of a coiled spring, he tore himself upward. His bare feet planted onto the chair, and in one seamless, gravity-defying leap, he vaulted onto the centre of the interrogation table.
